What you should know.

GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of medications that support appetite regulation and metabolic function. Compounded GLP-1 medications are customized formulations prepared by a state-licensed compounding pharmacy based on a prescription from a licensed healthcare provider. These medications are created to meet individual patient needs, such as specific dosing or formulation preferences, as determined during a clinical evaluation. Treatment is prescribed only when a provider determines it is appropriate for the patient's unique health profile.

GLP-1 therapy may be appropriate for adults with a BMI of 27 or higher who are seeking medically supervised weight management. During your assessment, our clinical providers will review your complete health profile — including medical history, current medications, and health goals — to determine if this protocol is right for you. Not every patient will qualify, and your safety always comes first.
Common side effects of GLP-1 receptor agonists may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and injection-site reactions. These tend to be mild and often improve over time as your body adjusts. Your provider will discuss potential risks and side effects specific to your health profile before prescribing. If you experience severe or concerning symptoms, contact your healthcare provider immediately.

  • Public Health and Safety: We may use and disclose your PHI to prevent or control disease, injury, or disability, to report child abuse or neglect, to report reactions to medications or problems with products, and to notify persons who may have been exposed to a communicable disease or may be at risk of spreading a disease or condition.
